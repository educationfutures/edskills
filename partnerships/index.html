Partnerships

How to partner

We designed this platform to make sure tools created under World Bank projects do not disappear when a project ends. Partnerships are how we keep them alive and useful. By joining, you help extend the reach and impact of these global public goods.

There are several ways to get involved:

  • Continuity and scaling: Work with us to adopt and adapt tools in new regions so they remain active beyond their original projects.
  • Universities and training providers: Host programs, train teachers, and embed tools in curricula.
  • Foundations and private sector partners: Provide funding or technical support to translate, localize, and sustain the tools.
  • IFC and development partners: Align with public–private initiatives that expand adoption and ensure sustainability.
  • Evidence and certification partners: Support quality assurance through research, evaluation, and ambassador programs.

Current partners

This effort builds on collaboration with ministries of education, universities, and NGOs in Romania, Armenia, Kosovo, Ukraine, and Moldova. The World Bank Group remains a key partner for continuity and public–private collaboration, while universities and local institutions ensure contextual relevance. As we expand, we will highlight active partners here and share stories of how they bring these tools to life.
