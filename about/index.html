About

Who we are

This initiative grew out of World Bank Group projects in Romania and the wider Europe and Central Asia region. Over the years, teams have created training tools, remedial education programs, digital skills curricula, and early warning modules. These were developed with public funds to serve as global public goods. Our goal is to make them accessible beyond the life of individual projects so they can continue to benefit teachers, students, and policymakers everywhere.

We operate as a collaborative platform that brings together World Bank staff, technical experts, educators, and partner organizations. By providing a single “home” for these tools, we reduce duplication, extend their reach, and support colleagues who want to adapt and scale them in new contexts.

Stewardship & governance

The World Bank and its partners steward the site as an open, non-commercial resource. A core coordination team manages the architecture, updates content, and ensures tools remain usable and accurate. Each product has a designated focal point—so visitors know whom to contact for questions, support, or adaptation guidance.

Governance relies on three principles:

  • Transparency: Each tool clearly identifies its origin, authors, and funding source.
  • Quality: Tools are vetted through a light certification process and, where possible, supported by evidence of effectiveness.
  • Continuity: When projects sunset, tools remain available here, preventing the loss of knowledge and resources.

We also work with IFC and other partners to align stewardship with public–private collaboration, fundraising opportunities, and regional adaptation.
