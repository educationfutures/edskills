Evidence: Remedial Math Program

Evidence and Case Studies: Remedial Math Program

The Remedial Math Program has been tested in Romania as part of efforts to support students at risk of falling behind in mathematics. Evidence from diagnostics and classroom observations highlights the urgent need for this type of structured remedial support.

Evidence from Diagnostics

  • BRIO baseline assessments (grades 5–8) consistently show weaknesses in real numbers (fractions, decimals), data and graph interpretation, and early algebraic reasoning.
  • These findings informed the design of the 10 learning units, ensuring they target the most common and persistent gaps.

Evidence from Teaching Practice

  • Teach classroom observations in Romania identified recurring challenges: limited feedback quality, few opportunities for deeper thinking, and gaps in promoting student autonomy and collaboration.
  • The program incorporates Foundational Teaching Skills (AFP) to strengthen teacher practice in exactly these areas, linking remedial math instruction to the World Bank’s TEACH framework.

Case Study: PNRAS Romania (2023–24)

The program was piloted in high-risk schools under Romania’s PNRAS (National Program for Supporting Students at Risk of School Dropout). Key features included:

  • Small-group instruction: 15–20 students per group over 30 weeks.
  • Teacher preparation: AFP modules covering 11 teaching skills (classroom climate and instructional practices).
  • Targeted design: Units built around diagnostic evidence, with a consistent lesson skeleton linking theory, guided practice, and exit checks.

Early pilot results indicate that students with the lowest baseline scores made measurable progress toward average performance levels. Teachers also reported greater confidence using structured feedback and interactive teaching strategies. Together, these outcomes point to the program’s potential to reduce reliance on private tutoring and improve retention in vulnerable schools.

Impact Statement

The Remedial Math Program demonstrates that pairing structured remedial lessons with teacher training and diagnostics can close skill gaps, keep vulnerable students engaged in school, and build pathways toward secondary-level math competence.
