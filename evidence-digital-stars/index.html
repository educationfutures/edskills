Evidence: Digital STARs

Evidence and Case Studies: Digital STARs Program

Digital STARs was launched to tackle Romania’s critical digital skills gap. With only 28% of citizens demonstrating basic digital skills (compared to 54% EU average), and adult participation in lifelong learning among the lowest in the EU, the program shows how large-scale community initiatives can close the divide.

Evidence from National Data

  • EU DESI Index: Romania ranks last in digital skills indicators, highlighting the urgency of intervention.
  • DigCompRo Framework: Newly developed national competence model (6 areas, 23 skills) provides the benchmark for STARs training and assessment.
  • Lifelong Learning: Adult participation in continuous education is 4.9%, the lowest in the EU, underscoring the importance of community-based programs.

Evidence from Program Pilots

  • Blended learning model: Pilot tests in libraries showed high engagement when theory was paired with real-life applications (e.g., using online health services, paying bills online).
  • Librarian-led facilitation: Feedback from early sessions confirmed that librarians are trusted mentors and effective local trainers.
  • Self-reflection surveys: Participants reported significant increases in confidence, especially in online safety, accessing e-government services, and using digital tools for employment.

Case Study: Libraries as Digital Hubs

In 2025, Digital STARs transformed over 1,100 libraries into learning hubs across all 42 counties of Romania. Each hub was equipped with modern computers, high-speed internet, and trained facilitators. Librarians ran blended courses for vulnerable groups—older adults, women, Roma communities, and rural residents—helping them acquire digital skills relevant to daily life.

One library in Vrancea County, for example, enrolled over 120 learners in its first six months. Pensioners reported using online health platforms for the first time, while unemployed youth learned to create digital CVs and apply for jobs online.

Impact Statement

The program demonstrates that combining national standards (DigCompRo), modernized libraries, and a cascading training-of-trainers model can rapidly scale digital inclusion. With a target of 100,000 trained citizens by 2026, Digital STARs is on track to serve as a model for other countries seeking to close digital gaps and promote equitable participation in the digital economy.
