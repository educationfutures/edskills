Digital STARs

Note: These resources are currently available in the Romanian language.

The Digital STARs Program is Romania’s flagship initiative to close the digital divide. By 2026, it will train 100,000 citizens in basic digital skills, turning public libraries into community learning hubs. The program combines a life-centered curriculum, a blended learning model, and a nationwide training-of-trainers cascade to ensure no one is left behind in the digital transition.

Who this tool is for

  • Citizens with little or no digital skills, including women, youth, Roma, rural residents, pensioners, and the unemployed.
  • Librarians and local facilitators who deliver training as community mentors.
  • Schools, ministries, and NGOs seeking scalable models for digital inclusion.

What you get

  • Digital Skills Training: A 60-hour blended course aligned with Romania’s new DigCompRo framework, covering six key competence areas and 23 skills.
  • Life-centered curriculum: Three tracks – Personal Life, Civic Life, Professional Life – ensure skills are applied in everyday contexts.
  • Training-of-Trainers (ToT): A cascade model (20 master trainers → 700 librarians → 100,000 citizens) that empowers local mentors.
  • Modernized libraries: Over 1,100 libraries equipped with high-speed internet, computers, and trained staff as digital hubs.
  • Learning platform: A dedicated LMS with self-reflection tools, activities, and online resources to support hybrid learning.

How to use it

  1. Select and train librarians through the ToT program.
  2. Enroll citizens through public libraries and community outreach campaigns.
  3. Deliver the 60-hour blended course (online + in-person), applying modules to daily life events.
  4. Support learners with mentoring, hands-on practice, and peer-to-peer interaction.
  5. Monitor progress using built-in assessments and DigCompRo-based self-reflection questions.

For more detail, view the how-to guide for this program.

Time and effort

  • Setup: Train master trainers and librarians (Nov 2024–Apr 2025).
  • Instruction: Citizens complete 60 hours of training, paced flexibly across 2–3 months.
  • Support: Ongoing mentoring, coaching for librarians, and national webinars through June 2026.

Languages

  • Available in Romanian.
  • Adaptable to other languages with translation and localization.

Evidence and impact

Romania ranks last in the EU in basic digital skills (28% vs. 54% EU average). Adult participation in lifelong learning is also the lowest in the EU (4.9%). Digital STARs directly addresses this gap by equipping citizens with essential skills for personal, civic, and professional life. The program is part of Romania’s National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR), modernizing libraries in all 42 counties and strengthening employability, e-government use, and social inclusion.

Its scalable design makes it a model for other countries facing similar challenges.
