Remedial Math Program

Note: These resources are currently available in the Romanian language.

The Remedial Math Program gives students who are falling behind a second chance to succeed. Built in Romania and piloted in vulnerable schools, it combines teacher support, engaging digital lessons, and diagnostic assessments. The program targets middle- and lower-secondary students (grades 5–8) as well as those entering high school with weak math foundations, and helps them build essential skills step by step.

Who this tool is for

  • Students at risk of dropping out or underperforming in math, especially in grades 5–8.
  • Teachers working in rural and underserved schools.
  • Schools and ministries looking for scalable, low-cost remedial solutions.

What you get

  • Foundational Teaching Skills (AFP modules): Eleven core skills for effective classroom practice, aligned with the World Bank’s TEACH framework. These include checking for understanding, feedback, adaptation, and building positive classroom climate.
  • Student learning units: Ten structured units delivered over 30 weeks (approx. 45 hours), covering numbers, algebra, geometry, and data & probability. Each unit blends short theory blocks, guided practice, and quick checks for understanding.
  • Lesson skeleton and tutorials: Consistent templates that link diagnostics (e.g., BRIO) to instruction, ensuring every session follows a predictable, student-centered routine.
  • Diagnostic tool: Baseline and follow-up assessments (e.g., BRIO tests) that identify learning gaps from grades 5–8 and track progress.
  • Teacher resource kits: Guides, examples, and AI-supported materials to help teachers plan and deliver lessons effectively.

How to use it

  1. Run the diagnostic test to identify student needs and group them by skill gaps.
  2. Deliver the 10 learning units across 30 weeks (1.5–2 hours per week), using the lesson skeleton as your guide.
  3. Use the digital tutorials and the Relu avatar to demonstrate concepts and keep students engaged.
  4. Monitor progress with built-in assessments at the end of each unit, and regroup students when needed.
  5. Join webinars and training sessions to strengthen teacher practice and share experiences.

For more detail, view the "how-to" guide for this program.

Time and effort

  • Setup: Install program container on school servers or computers.
  • Instruction: 30 weeks of learning (approx. 45 hours total).
  • Support: 8 webinars offered during rollout.

Languages

  • Available in Romanian.
  • Adaptable to other languages with translation and subtitles.

Evidence and impact

Piloted in Romania under the PNRAS program, the Remedial Math Program directly addresses the skill gaps identified by national diagnostics and World Bank TEACH classroom observations.

BRIO assessments show that students struggle most with real numbers, data/graph interpretation, and early algebra. Teach classroom data highlight areas such as feedback, deeper thinking, and student collaboration. The program is designed to close these gaps through targeted instruction and consistent teaching practices.

Early results show that low-performing students can progress toward average performance levels, offering a more inclusive pathway through secondary education and reducing the need for private tutoring.
