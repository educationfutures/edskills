Teach RO

Note: These resources are currently available in the Romanian language.

TEACH is the World Bank’s classroom observation tool used worldwide to measure teaching practices and support professional development. It focuses on how teachers manage the classroom, deliver instruction, and engage students. By providing a structured framework for observation, TEACH helps generate feedback that improves the quality of teaching.

Teach RO  offers a practical tool and course for observing teaching in primary and preschool classrooms in Romania. The course focuses on core domains such as classroom climate and instruction, and is delivered on the Teach RO platform (teach.edu.ro).

In Romania, TEACH has been adapted into a four-module e-learning program that goes beyond observation to actively train teachers. The modules combine international best practices with local classroom examples, equipping educators with strategies for effective instruction, positive classroom management, and stronger student engagement. This version offers Romanian teachers both the global rigor of TEACH and practical, context-specific support for improving their daily practice.

Who it’s for

  • Teachers and school leaders in primary or preschool education
  • Methodologists, mentors, and inspectors who conduct classroom observations
  • Faculties of education and NGOs supporting early-years and primary pedagogy

What you learn

  • How to use a structured observation framework in real classrooms
  • How to document evidence on classroom climate and instructional practices
  • How to give clear, actionable feedback to teachers based on observed behaviors

Format

  • Online access via the Teach RO platform
  • Course content organized around observation domains (e.g., classroom climate, instruction)
  • Includes guidance, examples, and tools for use during live lessons

How to enroll

The live course is available at: teach.edu.ro/courses/teach-ro. Access is provided on the Teach RO platform.

Time and effort

The Teach RO course is designed to fit educators’ real schedules. Most participants complete it in 8–10 hours over two to three weeks, depending on how much classroom practice they choose to include.

  • Each module can be completed independently.
  • Observation activities can be integrated into regular class hours.
  • The course requires only basic digital skills and internet access.

The goal is to make professional reflection and feedback part of daily teaching, not an extra burden.

Languages

  • Available in Romanian.
  • English translation underway.
  • Can be adapted for other countries (piloted in Armenia, Ukraine, Kosovo).

Evidence and impact

Teach has been piloted across several countries and is grounded in the World Bank’s global framework for measuring teaching practices. In Romania, it provides a scalable model for teacher training, reaching thousands of educators through online delivery. Its indicators on classroom climate and instructional quality are used to:

  • Provide consistent, evidence-based feedback to teachers.
  • Support school improvement plans and teacher mentoring.
  • Build national data on teaching quality and learning environments.

Teachers and mentors who have applied the framework report clearer discussions about instruction, stronger professional trust, and visible improvement in student engagement.
