TEACH-Romania

Note: These resources are currently available in the Romanian language.

TEACH is the World Bank’s classroom observation tool used worldwide to measure teaching practices and support professional development. It focuses on how teachers manage the classroom, deliver instruction, and engage students. By providing a structured framework for observation, TEACH helps generate feedback that improves the quality of teaching.

In Romania, TEACH has been adapted into a six-module e-learning program that goes beyond observation to actively train teachers. The modules combine international best practices with local classroom examples, equipping educators with strategies for effective instruction, positive classroom management, and stronger student engagement. This version offers Romanian teachers both the global rigor of TEACH and practical, context-specific support for improving their daily practice.

Who it’s for

  • Pre-primary, primary, and secondary teachers.
  • Teacher trainers and mentors.
  • Ministries of education and NGOs implementing teacher professional development programs.

What you get

  • Six self-paced modules: Covering key dimensions of teaching quality.
  • Observation framework: Structured tool for assessing classroom practices.
  • Case-based examples: Videos of classroom teaching to illustrate good practice.
  • Certification pathway: Two synchronous sessions that supplement e-learning and provide validation.
  • Adaptable content: Modules can be localized with translation and subtitles.

How to use it

  1. Register teachers or cohorts to complete the six online modules.
  2. Use the observation tool to assess classroom practice in a standardized way.
  3. Provide teachers with targeted feedback based on observed dimensions.
  4. Offer synchronous sessions for certification and deeper engagement.
  5. Scale through training-of-trainers or institutional integration.

For more detail, view the "how-to" guide for this program.

Time and effort

  • Self-paced modules: 15–20 hours in total.
  • Certification: 2 synchronous sessions (approx. 4 hours).
  • Observation practice: Ongoing, integrated into school routines.

Languages

  • Available in Romanian.
  • English translation underway.
  • Can be adapted for other countries (piloted in Armenia, Ukraine, Kosovo).

Evidence and impact

Teach has been piloted across several countries and is grounded in the World Bank’s global framework for measuring teaching practices. In Romania, it provides a scalable model for teacher training, reaching thousands of educators through online delivery.
